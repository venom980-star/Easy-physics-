KINEMATICS (Complete SKC Notes)

1. Kinematics Overview

Kinematics = Study of motion without considering its cause (force/mass).

✔️ Key Point:
  • Only focuses on Position, Velocity, and Acceleration.
  • No dynamics (force/mass) involved.

2. Classification of Motion

  • One-Dimensional (1D): Straight-line motion (e.g., train on track).
  • Two-Dimensional (2D): Planar (e.g., projectile, circular).
  • Three-Dimensional (3D): Full space (e.g., flight of bird).
Motion type ↔ choice of coordinate system & formulas.

3. Distance vs Displacement

  • Distance (scalar): Total path length.
  • Displacement (vector): Straight-line distance from start to end.
⚡ Remember: Displacement ≤ Distance always (unless the path is straight).

4. Speed & Velocity

  • Average Speed = total distance / total time
  • Average Velocity = total displacement / total time = Δx/Δt
  • Instantaneous Velocity = lim (Δt→0) Δx/Δt = dx/dt
v = dx/dt
🔍 Velocity can be negative; watch direction vs speed!

5. Acceleration

  • Average Acceleration = Δv/Δt
  • Instantaneous Acceleration = dv/dt
a = dv/dt
Acceleration sign indicates speeding up (same direction) or slowing down (opposite direction).

6. Equations of Motion (Uniform Acceleration)

v = u + at
s = ut + ½ a t²
v² = u² + 2as
📌 When to Use:
  • Always when a = constant.
  • Choose based on known & unknown variables.

7. Derivation Tips — Quick!

➕ From v = u + at → t = (v−u)/a. Substitute into s = ut + ½at² to derive s = (u+v)/2 · t.
➕ y = ut + ½at² also gives v² = u² + 2as by eliminating t.

8. Graphical representation

  • x–t Graph: slope = velocity
  • v–t Graph: slope = acceleration; area under = displacement
  • a–t Graph: area under = change in velocity
Graph interpretation Qs are easy marks—practice with straight & curved plots!

9. Average vs Instantaneous Summary

  • avg v = (x₂ − x₁)/(t₂ − t₁)
  • inst v = lim Δt→0 Δx/Δt
  • avg a = (v₂ − v₁)/(t₂ − t₁)
  • inst a = lim Δt→0 Δv/Δt

10. Relative Motion (1D)

Velocity of A relative to B:

VAB = VA – VB
🔄 Always subtract in correct order—direction matters!

11. Non-Uniform Motion

If acceleration isn’t constant, use calculus:

v = ∫ a dt, x = ∫ v dt = ∫ ∫ a dt²
SKC skips detailed calculus—stick to uniform acceleration for exam mains.

12. Important Tips & Tricks

  • Draw a motion diagram with positive/negative direction.
  • θ = 0°, +ve direction → a positive acceleration increases speed.
  • Common errors: wrong sign, mis-read graph, wrong eqn usage.
🧠 SKC Shortcut: If a < 0 and u > 0 → slowing down. When v becomes zero, object reverses direction.

13. Sample Problem (SKC Style)

Q. A car starts from rest, accelerates uniformly at 2 m/s² for 5 s. Find distance covered.

A. u = 0, a = 2, t = 5
Use s = ut + ½ at² → s = 0 + ½×2×25 = 25 m

Check units! Time in seconds, acceleration in m/s² → s in meters ✔️

15. Final Tips for Exam

  • Always list knowns (u, a, t, s, v).
  • Underline what they want (e.g., "distance", "final velocity").
  • Choose equation with only 1 unknown.
  • Write units in m, s, m/s, m/s².
If stuck, draw x–t & v–t graph and use area/slope method ✔️
